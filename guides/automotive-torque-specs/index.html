Automotive Bolt Torque Specifications

General torque guidelines for common automotive fasteners. Always consult your vehicle's service manual for model-specific values.

Important: The values below are general guidelines based on bolt grade and size. Actual torque specifications vary by manufacturer, engine, and model year. Always refer to the factory service manual for your specific vehicle.

Automotive Torquing Best Practices

Use a calibrated torque wrench. Hand-feel is unreliable. Even experienced mechanics overestimate their accuracy. A click-type or digital torque wrench eliminates guesswork.

Thread condition matters. Rusty, damaged, or dry threads will absorb torque into friction rather than converting it to clamp load. Clean threads and apply the correct lubricant or thread sealer as specified.

Torque-to-yield (TTY) bolts are single-use. These bolts are tightened to a specific torque then turned an additional angle, permanently stretching the bolt. Cylinder head bolts, connecting rod bolts, and many modern suspension bolts are TTY. Reusing them risks failure.

Tightening sequence matters on multi-bolt patterns (heads, manifolds, valve covers). Always follow the manufacturer's specified pattern, typically starting from the center and working outward in a star or spiral pattern.
